Cannon Residence

Hampstead, London

A Victorian house conversion shaped around light, depth, and generous family living.

Hampstead, London
Victorian conversion + basement extension
Architecture, Interiors, and Construction
3,300 sqft

Design Brief

Cannon Residence reimagines a Victorian shell into a contemporary family home. The concept balances openness and intimacy while preserving the character of the original structure.

Our focus was to create flowing volumes, precise natural lighting, and a calm material palette that allows architectural moments to stand out.

Architecture

The central staircase acts as a vertical light tunnel, linking all levels and creating a shifting play of shadows through the day.

A dramatic void between lower ground and ground floor introduces scale while maintaining a warm, livable atmosphere.

Interior Design

Soft neutral walls amplify daylight while timber textures introduce tactility and warmth. The result is gallery-like yet comfortable for daily family life.

Brass, marble, and custom joinery provide carefully edited contrast, turning functional zones into crafted moments.

Public living spine with dining, kitchen, and reception arranged around a central axis.

Award Recognition

The Sunday Times British Homes Awards

  • Winner - Best Interior Design
  • Highly Commended - Home Transformation

Furniture + Joinery

Bespoke pieces were designed for each room to maintain spatial continuity, from kitchen islands to storage walls and fitted bedroom systems.

Each item was proportioned to the architecture, so furniture feels integrated rather than layered on top.
